Living tobacco-free:Healthy people

Smoke from the burning end of a cigarette has more harmful chemicals in it than the smoke inhaled directly by the person who is smoking it. There is no safe level of exposure to second-hand smoke.

Infants and children are particularly at risk to the effects of second-hand smoke. The children that are exposed to second-hand smoke are at greater risk for Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS), pneumonia, bronchitis and asthma.
